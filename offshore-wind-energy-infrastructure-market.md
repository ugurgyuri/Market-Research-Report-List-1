<p><p>Offshore wind energy infrastructure comprises various foundation types that support wind turbines in marine environments. Monopile foundations consist of a single, large steel pole driven into the seabed, ideal for shallow waters. Floating foundations utilize a buoyant structure anchored to the seabed, making them suitable for deeper waters. Other market types may include jacket foundations, gravity-based structures, and tension-leg platforms, each designed to optimize stability, cost-efficiency, and energy production in diverse offshore conditions.</p></p>
