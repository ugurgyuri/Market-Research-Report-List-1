<p><p>The food enzymology market includes specialized enzymes like beta-glucanase, xylanase, pectinase, cellulase, α-amylase, and neutral protease, each serving distinct functions in food processing. Beta-glucanase breaks down beta-glucans in grains, enhancing fermentation and digestibility. Xylanase targets hemicellulose in plant materials, improving texture. Pectinase assists in fruit processing, increasing juice yield, while cellulase breaks down cellulose for better fiber utilization. α-Amylase aids in starch conversion, essential for brewing and baking. Neutral protease enhances protein modification, improving taste and texture in various foods.</p></p>
